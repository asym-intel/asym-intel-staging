    What GMM Is

    The Global Macro Monitor (GMM) is a weekly structured macro-financial intelligence product published by Asymmetric Intelligence. It tracks six strategic domains — sovereign debt dynamics, banking and credit stress, market structure risk, real economy indicators, composite financial stress indices, and systemic amplifiers — and synthesises them into a single system stress label with regime conviction every Monday at 08:00 UTC.

    GMM is one monitor in the Asymmetric Intelligence hub-and-spoke architecture — a network of specialised monitors, each covering a distinct strategic domain, sharing a common design system and coordinated publication schedule. Cross-monitor flags are published weekly alongside the full GMM report.

    GMM measures macro-financial risk on a consistent indicator-by-indicator basis each week, scoring each indicator as WARNING (−1.0), ELEVATED (−0.3), or GREEN (+1.0) against published crisis thresholds. The system average score drives the system stress label. No arbitrary editorial overrides are applied to scores without a documented blind spot override rule.

    The primary audience is analysts, portfolio managers, policy-oriented readers, and intelligence professionals who need structured macro-financial signal rather than news narrative.

    Source Hierarchy

    GMM uses a named five-tier source hierarchy. Sources are always cited at the indicator level. Higher tiers take precedence. Downgrading to a lower tier requires a documented reason.

    TierSourcesRule
    T1 BIS, IMF, World Bank, Federal Reserve, ECB — official publications, statistical releases, working papers, financial stability reports, and regulatory data Always use. Link directly to primary data release, never to press coverage.
    T2 National statistical agencies, central banks (non-T1), treasury departments, BLS, BEA, ONS, Eurostat, BoJ, FINRA, CBOE, FRED, TIC data Use when T1 source does not publish the specific series.
    T3 Specialised financial data providers: Trepp (CRE), IIF (global debt), PIIE (trade), BofA FMS (positioning), Cass Information Systems (freight), ISM, University of Michigan (sentiment) Use for proprietary datasets not covered by T1/T2. Always link to primary report.
    T4 High-quality financial press and research: Reuters, Bloomberg, FT, Goldman Sachs Global Investment Research, Morgan Stanley Research Use only when no T1–T3 source covers the item. Flagged in the indicator record.
    T5 Specialised data aggregators and market structure sources: Windward (AIS/shipping), TradingEconomics (for secondary confirmation only) Last resort. Always flagged. Cross-checked against T1–T3 where possible.
    Signal Scoring Criteria
    Indicator Scoring

    Each indicator is scored weekly against a published crisis threshold:

    ScoreFlagCondition
    −1.0WARNINGIndicator at or beyond crisis threshold. Immediate systemic concern.
    −0.3ELEVATEDIndicator deteriorating, approaching crisis threshold, or structurally strained.
    +1.0GREENIndicator within normal range. No systemic signal.

    Direction multipliers may adjust the raw score within its tier. A WARNING indicator with an "easing" direction receives a direction multiplier of 0.9×, not a full reclassification.

    System Average Score

    The system average score is the unweighted mean of all indicator scores in the current issue. It drives the system stress label. No domain is up- or down-weighted relative to another. All 25+ indicators contribute equally.

    Asset Class Scoring

    Eight asset classes are scored weekly: Consumer Staples, Energy, EM Equities, Real Estate, Metals, Bonds, Crypto, and Tech. Each asset class score is the domain-weighted composite of the indicators most directly affecting that asset class. Asset class scores range from −1.0 (BEARISH) to +1.0 (BULLISH).

    What Stress Labels Mean

    The system stress label is derived from the system average score and the directional momentum across domains. Labels are:

    LabelScore RangeMeaning
    LOW +0.5 to +1.0 No material systemic stress. Normal macro environment. Most indicators GREEN.
    GUARDED 0.0 to +0.5 Minor pockets of stress. One or two ELEVATED indicators. No immediate systemic threat.
    MODERATE −0.3 to 0.0 Multiple ELEVATED indicators. Systemic stress building. Regime conviction may not yet be HIGH.
    ELEVATED −0.5 to −0.3 Several WARNING indicators or widespread ELEVATED signals. Regime confirmed. Tactical positioning warranted.
    HIGH −0.7 to −0.5 Multiple WARNING indicators across domains. Systemic risk acute. Crisis threshold breaches confirmed.
    CRITICAL Below −0.7 Systemic crisis conditions. Most indicators at or beyond crisis thresholds. Immediate risk of cascading failures.

    Composite labels such as "ELEVATED → HIGH" indicate that the score is at the boundary between two tiers and directional momentum points toward the higher tier. They signal that the next issue is likely to show a label change if the trajectory continues.

    Blind Spot Override Framework

    Certain indicators contain structural distortions that make their raw value misleading. GMM maintains a named catalogue of blind spot rules. When a rule is active, the indicator record is flagged and the warning is published in the report. Scores are not changed automatically — blind spot overrides are informational flags that require the human editor to apply a documented adjustment.

    Rule 1 — Earnings Suppression

    Earnings revision scores may be suppressed by share buyback distortion and non-GAAP adjustments. When Rule 1 is active on the earnings_revisions indicator, the score is flagged as potentially overstated. Watch Q2 guidance season for real-earnings confirmation.

    Rule 2 — Nominal M2 Mirage

    Nominal M2 growth can mislead when real M2 (deflated by Core PCE or PPI pipeline) is flat or negative. When Rule 2 is active on m2_money_supply, the nominal figure is flagged and the real M2 calculation is published alongside the nominal reading. Affected asset class: Crypto (narrative thinning in real terms).

    Regime Framework

    GMM identifies the prevailing macro regime each week. The regime is the structural backdrop within which the stress label is interpreted. Current regime definitions:

    RegimeConditionsAsset Implication
    STAGFLATION Persistent above-target inflation + sub-trend growth + tightening financial conditions. All asset classes negatively biased. No bullish asset class; defensive positioning only. Duration, EM, and real estate most exposed.
    GOLDILOCKS Inflation at or below target + above-trend growth + easing financial conditions. Broad bullish bias. Equities outperform; duration extends. Risk assets perform.
    REFLATION Rising inflation with recovering growth + monetary easing still in place or beginning. Commodities, energy, and cyclicals outperform. Short duration.
    DEFLATION BUST Falling inflation with contracting growth. Credit stress acute. Demand collapse. Long duration, gold, and cash. Equities deeply negative. Credit spreads blow out.

    Regime conviction levels (LOW, MEDIUM, HIGH) reflect how many of the regime's structural preconditions are simultaneously confirmed. A HIGH conviction regime requires confirmation across at least four of the six domains.
