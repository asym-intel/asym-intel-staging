About This Monitor

The European Strategic Autonomy Monitor is a weekly open-source intelligence publication tracking Europe's capacity to act independently. It synthesises evidence on EU defence investment, hybrid warfare, institutional developments, and member state alignment across six dimensions of strategic autonomy: energy, defence, technology, finance, materials, and institutions.

The monitor applies an analytical framework developed from ECFR, IISS, and Chatham House methodologies, tracking both the structural drivers of dependence and the policy responses designed to reduce it. Each issue provides a lead signal, module-by-module reporting, and a cross-monitor flag layer connecting ESA developments to related Asymmetric Intelligence trackers.

Publishing Schedule

Frequency: Weekly

Publish time: Wednesday 19:00 UTC

Coverage window: Seven days ending on the Wednesday of publication

First issue: Volume 1, Issue 1 — 30 March 2026

Issues are numbered sequentially within each volume year. The monitor publishes continuously with no scheduled breaks.

Editorial

The European Strategic Autonomy Monitor is produced by the Asymmetric Intelligence research team. Analysis is drawn from open sources including official EU and NATO documentation, think-tank research, European Parliament proceedings, and verified news reporting.

All source links are included in the published JSON and HTML output. Severity assessments follow the shared Asymmetric Intelligence rating framework: Critical / High / Moderate / Positive.
