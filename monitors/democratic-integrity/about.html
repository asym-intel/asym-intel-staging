About This Monitor

01

What Is This?

The World Democracy Monitor (WDM) is a weekly intelligence publication tracking the structural health of democratic institutions across a monitored set of countries. It is part of the Asymmetric Intelligence network of monitors.

Each weekly issue synthesises primary-source reporting from organisations including V-Dem, Freedom House, CIVICUS, Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch, IPI, RSF, OSCE/ODIHR, IDEA, and others into a structured intelligence brief covering:

  • A Country Heatmap — countries in Rapid Decay, Recovery, and Watch List tiers with severity scores
  • Intelligence Items — ranked significant developments with full sourcing
  • Institutional Integrity Flags — leading indicators of systemic erosion in key institutions (judiciary, civil service, electoral administration, intelligence community)
  • Mimicry Chains — documented spread of specific authoritarian legislative templates across countries
  • Cross-monitor flags — connections to other Asymmetric Intelligence monitors (FIMI, Strategic Conflict, AI Governance, Environmental Risks, European Strategic Autonomy, Global Macro)

The WDM takes an explicitly analytical position: democratic backsliding is a structural, measurable phenomenon with documented leading indicators, not a matter of opinion. The methodology draws on the academic frameworks developed by V-Dem, IDEA, Freedom House, and Democratic Erosion Consortium researchers.
