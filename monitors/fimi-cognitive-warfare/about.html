About the FIMI & Cognitive Warfare Monitor

Description

The FIMI & Cognitive Warfare Monitor tracks Foreign Information Manipulation and Interference (FIMI) and cognitive warfare operations across all state and near-state actors. Unlike frameworks that restrict attribution to Russia and China, this monitor applies consistent analytical criteria across all actors — Russia, China, Iran, Gulf states (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar), Israel, US-linked entities, and commercial cognitive warfare operators such as Black Cube, NSO Group, and Psy-Group (legacy).

The monitor is structured around three analytical layers: active campaigns (discrete, attributed operations with clear objectives and infrastructure), actor doctrine profiles (persistent doctrinal and organisational context carried forward across issues), and platform enforcement tracking (documenting the asymmetric enforcement environment across Meta, Google, X, and other platforms). The key analytical distinction is between operations that have been Confirmed (third-party institutional attribution), High (strong circumstantial evidence), Assessed (analytical judgement), and Possible (early-stage hypothesis).

The monitor explicitly tracks the convergence problem: the blurring boundary between US-registered political actors with European far-right networks with Kremlin-aligned infrastructure. Standard single-actor FIMI frameworks are structurally poorly equipped to capture this convergence. This monitor applies hub-and-spoke cross-referencing with the World Democracy Monitor and European Strategic Autonomy Monitor to track the full causal chain.
